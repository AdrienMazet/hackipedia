Cleopatra

Cleopatra VII Philopator was the last active ruler of the Ptolemaic Kingdom of Egypt. This mock article area is here to simulate the extension in context: dense Wikipedia content, long-form reading, and a small assistant entry point that opens a more playful but still serious way to explore historical figures.

The intent is not to replace the encyclopedia, but to create a faster entry into it. A reader lands on a page, taps the assistant bubble, and sees a concise card with the same logic every time: portrait, identity, tensions, legacy, and a direct bridge into conversation. The visuals shift with the subject so the interface feels authored rather than templated.

Cleopatra can evoke ceremonial gold, papyrus tones, and small hieroglyphic motifs without sliding into parody. Christopher Columbus can feel cartographic and weathered, with a map-room atmosphere. Ada Lovelace can move toward salon elegance and analytical notation. The structure stays stable while the cultural and historical cues change.

This file is designed as a playground for direction rather than finished production UI. It includes a simulated Wikipedia article, a floating extension trigger, a card selector, and a small chat entry experience with suggested prompts and free input.

The next step after visual validation would be to move this into a real extension surface, likely a content-script injected trigger plus an overlay or side panel that reads the current Wikipedia article and hydrates the card with live data.
